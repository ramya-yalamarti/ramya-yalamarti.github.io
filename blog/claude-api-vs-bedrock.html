Claude direct vs. Bedrock: a lock-in problem in disguise

by Ramya Yalamarthi · 2 min read

Six months in, I keep hearing the same sentence from teams building on Claude: "I wish we'd made the Bedrock vs direct API decision differently."

It's never about latency. It's about discovering — once the model is wired into authentication, observability, and the retry loop — that the comparison table they optimized against wasn't measuring the decision they were actually making.

Most teams pick this in an afternoon. The ones who redo it spend a quarter.

The feature bake-off is a decoy

Every comparison post lines up the same columns: cost per token, first-token latency, IAM support, observability integration.

Fine tables. Wrong frame.

You aren't picking features. You're picking which organization — Anthropic or AWS — gets to own the substrate of a system you plan to run for years. The feature list is what they hand you today. The lock-in is what you inherit forever.

Two lock-ins, priced differently

Direct API lock-in is Anthropic. Their pricing model. Their deprecation timeline. Their rate limits. Their TOS. All negotiable only to the degree they choose.

Bedrock lock-in is AWS. Their primitives — IAM, VPC, KMS, CloudTrail, AgentCore, SigV4. Their release cadence (Bedrock lags direct by weeks on new Claude features). Their regional rollout. Their margin.

Neither is worse. Both are real. The useful question isn't which lock-in exists — it's which one you've already paid for.

The heuristic

If your company lives on AWS, Bedrock lock-in is already paid. You made that bet years ago, and the new integration inherits governance you've already built. Going direct adds a second strategic vendor to a stack that didn't have one.

If your company is multi-cloud or cloud-light, direct API means one vendor relationship instead of two. Walking away from Anthropic is painful but bounded. Walking away from AWS is a corporate event.

Same decision. Different answer. Environment makes the call.

What I ask now

When someone asks "Bedrock or direct?", I don't answer in tokens or milliseconds.

What are you already locked into, and are you ready to make a second long-term bet?

Pick the bet you can live with.
